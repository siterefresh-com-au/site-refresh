Before we begin

Useful questions, plainly answered.

A clear start makes the visit more useful. Photos and a simple description are often enough.

What should I send with an enquiry?

Tell us what has changed, where it is happening and whether the problem is constant or intermittent. For planned work, a room photo or marked-up plan can help establish the first conversation.

Can you help before renovation work starts?

Yes. Early electrical planning is particularly useful for appliance loads, switch locations, lighting layers, data points and anything that may be difficult to add once walls and cabinetry are complete.

Do you quote before carrying out the work?

The scope and next step should be agreed before work begins. Faults sometimes need diagnostic testing first; in that case, the diagnostic stage and what follows can be explained separately.

What happens if you find something unexpected?

The work pauses at the decision point. The issue, options and likely effect on the agreed scope are explained before any additional work proceeds.
